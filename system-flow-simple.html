The 4 Players

Role What they do
Contractor (Boulder) Buys materials, pays workers, bills the Owner monthly. Tracks profit/loss internally.
Owner (Prevail) Hired the Contractor. Pays monthly based on % of work done. Holds back 10% retainage until project ends.
Architect (Maust) Independent check. Confirms work is actually done before Owner pays.
Accountant (Summit) Helps Contractor reconcile books — makes sure money received matches money spent.

Two Key Documents

  • Draw (external) — Monthly invoice the Contractor sends to the Owner. Says: "work is 85% done, you owe us $723K this month."
  • CFR — Cost-to-Finish Report (internal) — Contractor's private view. Says: "we spent $2.1M, Owner paid us $1.9M, we're $200K down on Division 8."

Simple rule: When Contractor opens a Draw, they also see CFR on the side. When Owner opens a Draw, they see only Draw — never CFR. This protects the Contractor's internal costs and profit margin from being seen by the Owner.
Role Opens Draw Opens CFR
Contractor ✓ sees Draw + CFR ✓ sees CFR only
Owner ✓ sees Draw only ✗ blocked
Architect ✓ sees Draw only ✗ no access
Accountant ✓ sees Draw ✓ sees CFR

Why retainage?

Owner holds back 10% of every payment until the project is fully done. This is their safety net — if the Contractor walks away or work is bad, Owner has money to fix it. Once Contractor finishes, retainage gets released as the final draw.
