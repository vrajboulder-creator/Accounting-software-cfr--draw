CFR vs Draw — Who Sees What & When?

Understanding the relationship between the two documents and who can view each.

The Key Rule

CFR contains Draws, not the other way around.

CFR = contractor's full financial picture (every cost + every payment received).
Draw = one monthly bill sent to the owner (part of CFR's data).

So: CFR view shows everything. Draw view shows only the billing side — but contractor can peek at CFR context while viewing a draw.

Why Owner can't see CFR

Analogy: You hire a contractor to build your house for $500K fixed price. The contractor's real cost is $420K — they make $80K profit. If you saw their CFR, you'd see the $80K margin and demand a discount. That's why fixed-price contracts hide internal costs.

The owner pays based on: scheduled value (agreed budget per division) × % completed — not on what the contractor actually spent.

What the CFR side-pane on a Draw shows

When contractor opens a Draw, a collapsible side-panel shows CFR data:

  • Per-division gross spend (real costs from transactions)
  • % of budget used with red bars for over-budget divisions
  • This draw's "this period" amount overlaid per division
  • Lets contractor verify: "am I billing in line with what I actually spent?"

Numbers must tie out

If CFR and Draw don't match, something is wrong:

Per division:
CFR Gross Spend ≈ G703 col G (Total Completed)
CFR Net Received = G702 Line 6 cumulative
CFR Retainage = G703 col I

Cash balance check:
Spend − Retainage − Received ≈ $0 (reconciled)
If > $0: owner owes contractor
If < $0: contractor billed more than spent (prepayment / overbilled)

Summary in one sentence

Draw = external billing doc (Owner sees it).
CFR = internal financial truth (Owner never sees it).
Contractor can see both simultaneously when reviewing a Draw, to verify their billing matches reality.
